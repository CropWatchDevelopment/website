## Design Context

### Users

Potential new CropWatch customers and the decision makers responsible for cold-storage operations, food safety, quality assurance, facilities, and compliance. They are evaluating whether automated monitoring can reduce manual recording, identify temperature problems earlier, support audits, and remain dependable during communication or equipment failures.

### Brand Personality

Precise, dependable, and approachable. The site should build confidence through concrete operational details and verifiable product capabilities without sounding overly promotional or making unsupported claims.

### Aesthetic Direction

A clear, light, professional Japanese B2B marketing experience using CropWatch's navy, sapphire, emerald, and gold design system. Content should follow the established agriculture page rhythm: focused section introductions, concise feature grids, evidence-led split layouts, real deployment imagery, structured specifications, and practical FAQs. Preserve the existing cold-chain hero. Avoid gradients in new work, decorative visual noise, generic dashboard aesthetics, and excessive card nesting.

### Design Principles

1. Lead with the operational outcome, then provide the technical evidence that supports it.
2. Help decision makers scan and compare capabilities through consistent, clearly labeled sections.
3. Separate available capabilities from planned features and avoid overstating certification or compliance claims.
4. Reuse shared layout primitives and design tokens to keep pages maintainable and visually consistent.
5. Maintain accessible contrast, reduced-motion support, and responsive layouts without horizontal scrolling from 375px upward.
